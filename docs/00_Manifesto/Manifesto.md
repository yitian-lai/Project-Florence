## Why Florence Exists
Florence exists because clinicians should spend their attention caring for patients, not trying to identify the most important signal hidden within overwhelming clinical data. Modern healthcare generates more information than any clinician can reasonably process in the moment. Florence exists not to replace clinical judgment, but to ensure that the most important signals are never buried beneath the noise.

## What We Believe
- Explainability is a necessity

Doctors should never be asked to trust a prediction without understanding why it was made. Florence is designed to present the reasoning, supporting clinical indicators, and confidence behind every recommendation, enabling clinicians to make informed decisions rather than blindly accepting AI outputs.

- When AI makes a mistake, doctors can see it and correct it

Something that never makes a mistake can actually be unsettling. Florence is designed to allow clinicians to review, challenge, and correct AI outputs. The sense of "controllability" inspires more trust than mere "perfection." Because trust grows when humans remain in control.

- Integrate into the workflow rather than adding an extra step

Florence should fit naturally into existing clinical workflows rather than requiring clinicians to adapt to the technology. Because doctors simply wouldn't use it when things got busy but if it operates as part of their daily workflow, then it ceases to be an extra burden and becomes a silent partner.


## What We Will Never Do
- Florence will never make clinical decisions on behalf of a physician.
- Florence will never hide why a prediction is made.
- Florence will never optimise for accuracy at the cost of transparency.

## Our Promise
We promise to build technology that helps clinicians focus on what matters most, while ensuring that every recommendation remains transparent, explainable, and under human control. 

\
_This manifesto is a living document. As Project Florence evolves through research, engineering, and clinical understanding, this manifesto may be refined. However, its core belief will remain unchanged: **Technology should strengthen clinical judgment, never replace it.**_
